Endangered Species:
Captured, Killed, and Sold.
The ways in which illegal poachers use endangered animals parts.
By Taylor Horn

Whether it be for personal or economic gain, endangered species are continuing to be poached for mass profit across the globe. According to the World Animal Foundation, around 20 billion dollars is made off the illegal trade of endangered species, with tens of millions of species being captured each year. Data gathered from the Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species (CITES), in 2016 shows that only 1755 endangered species were seized from other countries that were trying to import an endangered species into the US. This small percentage of recovered imports from the millions of endangered species traded gives us an insight into what species are most traded, what the most common use for them would be, and which countries are importing the most into the United States.

Below is a look at some of the most imported endangered species into the U.S. and what they are commonly used for.
Clams and corals

Sea creatures such as clams and corals dominate the endangered species trade market. There were 192 instances of corals being imported into the United States illegally and 42 instances of clams. This accounts for 13% of all of the seized species that were illegally imported in 2016.

Alligators

69 alligators were confiscated before being illegally imported into the United States in 2016. 54 of the 69 were leather products, most likely intended for lucrative fashion industry.

Bears

In 2016, 50 endangered bears were attempted to be imported into the U.S. These included American black bears, Asian black bears, sun bears, and brown bears. Even polar bears were illegally poached for import. They are used for a variety of personal purposes and are used as hunting trophies.

Crocodiles

Similarly to alligators, crocodiles are illegally imported for fashion purposes. There were 114 instances of endangered crocodiles being confiscated before being illegally imported into the U.S. in 2016

Deer

Deer such as the Siberian musk deer and the Indian hog deer were imported for personal uses. There were 24 instances of confiscated deer in 2016.

Elephants

The illegal trade of elephant parts is more widely covered in the media, since so many are poached for their valuable tusks. The 97 instances were for a wide range of uses such as circuses, trophies, law enforcement, and other commercial uses.

Hawks

Poached mainly for their feathers, 18 hawks were confiscated from illegal trade into the U.S. This includes red-tailed hawks, sharp-shinned hawks, red-shouldered hawks, Cooper's hawks, and more.

Iguanas

Exported primarily from Mexico for their meat, iguanas are also one of the most widely poached endangered species, with 39 instances of confiscated iguanas from exporters in 2016.

Leopards

Leopards are poached for their furs which are used in the fashion industry, and they are poached for the use of hunting trohpies. 13 were seized before being illegally imported into the U.S. two years ago.

Lizards

Like alligators and crocodiles, lizards are illegally imported for their leathery skin. In 2016, some were even imported while still being alive.

Orchids

There were 57 instances of orchids being seized from being imported. Imported alive or as medicine, large quantities of orhcids were attempted to be imported. In one case, 68.48kg of powder from orchids was confiscated.

Parrots

Parrots were illegally imported for their feathers. Out of the 22 instances in 2016, there was one case were 130 parrot feathers were attempted to be imported into the United States.

Peafowls

On a more extreme level than parrots, thousands of peafowl feathers are attempted to be imported at times. Their feathers are for commercial use, such as the fashion industry.

Pythons

Pythons, as with most endangered reptiles, are poached for their leather. Out of the 138 instances of their capture, 131 for their leathery skins.

Seahorses

The bodies of seahorses are exported personal use mainly, but in some cases seahorses are exported for medicinal purposes. There were 30 instances of seahorses being illegally imported into the U.S. in 2016.

Sea Turtles and Tortoises

There were 88 instances of sea turtles and tortoises being smuggled into the U.S. They're used for their shells, meat, as medicine, jewlery, oil, and more.

Sturgeons

Caviar is the main use for sturgeons, a type of fish, which were involved in 75 instances of illegal importing into the United States.

Whales

Another animal that has received more media coverage for being poached on a mass scale is whales. The majority of the 47 instances of being smuggled into the U.S. was for the whale's meat.







The United States Fish and Wildlife Service provides an in depth look at the general purpose of every imported endangered species. These are classified as the following: breeding in captivity or artificial propagation, law enforcement/judicial/forensic use only, scientific, educational, biomedical research, commercial, botanic gardens, personal, reintroduction/introduction into the wild, hunting trophies, circuses/traveling exhibitions, and zoos. In 2016, personal and commercial purposes dominated the list from the confiscated endangered species.


Despite active efforts for reform from the USFWS such as The Endangered Species Act and captive breeding, which is breeding animals in a safe and controlled environment such as a zoo, we still have long ways to go before we will start to see the downfall of the illegal trade of endangered speicies.
