How The United States
Endangers Sea Turtles
Despite being a large, developed country, the U.S. still imports endangered animals,
bringing in 97 sea turtles in 2016 alone.
By Taylor Horn

In recent years the endangerment of sea turtles has come to the publics' attention. With the World Wildlife Fund claiming that all 7 species of sea turtles are at risk or critically endangered, programs like the Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species (CITES) have been taken on the challenge of reversing man-made damage to endangered species.



Yet, even in 2016, there were 97 instances where sea turtles from across the globe were being brought into The United States illegally and were confiscated. Many people are unaware of how often sea turtles are attempted to be imported into the United States because they believe the illegal trade happens only in obscure, foreign contries. The reality is that the illegal trade of endangered species happens in most countries, including large, developed countries like the United States.




Out of the 97 instances of sea turltles attempted to be imported into the U.S.,
9 of the instances were exports from Mexico, more than any other coutnry that year.

Sea turtle parts are collected illegally for a variety of different reasons. Their meat can be used for human consumptions, their shells for making trinkets, jewlery, and other decorations, and their eggs can be used for consumption or medicinal purposes. Sea turtle eggs are considered a delicacy in many countries.

Tens of thousands of sea turtles are poched each year for their body parts. Despite efforts by CITES to get all countries to stop the illegal trade of sea turtles, the countries have to voluntarily comit to the agreement. This means that many countries who profit off of selling sea turtle parts do not have to sign the CITES agreement and can continue to exploit the turtles.





Environmental protection programs have been working to help countries use sea turtles to bring in tourism money that would be more profitable than selling the turtles illegally. Until more people become aware of the endangerment of sea turtles and countries rather sign the CITES agreement or switch to using sea turtles for tourism, we may not have these creatures for much longer.
